TL;DR · The campaign in 30 seconds

The brand: Pacific Airways — a regional SEA carrier defending share against budget competitors on the Bali route.
The ask: Drive bookings during a 3-week sale window (Apr 15 – May 7, 2026) targeting travel-intent users in MY/SG/TH.
The goal: 12M impressions, 4.5× ROAS in-app, plus measurable off-site bookings on the airline's own site.
The result: 12.4M impressions delivered, 6.2× ROAS in-app, 9.4× combined with off-site, $48K spend → ~$450K in attributed revenue.
Section 1 · The scenario

Why Pacific Airways ran this campaign

In early April 2026, Pacific Airways approached Aeon360 with a tactical brief. Budget carriers had aggressively undercut their pricing on Kuala Lumpur ↔ Denpasar routes through Q1, and the airline had a 3-week window to defend share before peak summer travel kicked off in June.

Pacific's marketing team had three constraints. First, budget was tight — $48K USD for the full campaign, modest by airline standards. Second, the team needed measurable bookings, not just brand impressions; their CFO had been skeptical of digital ad spend without closed-loop reporting. Third, the campaign needed to reach actual travel intenders, not the broad lifestyle audience their previous social campaigns had attracted.

Aeon360's commerce media estate fit the brief: a captive audience of high-intent users already in a transactional mindset, with placement options in the Travel category and on the home hero carousel. Crucially, Aeon360 could close the attribution loop — both for in-app conversions and (via a server-side conversion API) for bookings completed on Pacific Airways' own site.

Section 3 · The setup

The mechanical details

Before reading the dashboard, it helps to understand what was actually deployed:

  • Placements: The home hero carousel (top of feed, premium inventory) plus a sponsored tile in the Travel category. The hero carousel rotates and Pacific Airways got slot 1 of 4 for the campaign duration.
  • Creatives: Two variants ran in parallel rotation. Variant A led with the price ("From RM 388") on a deep-navy gradient. Variant B led with the destination shot on a softer grey palette. Aeon360's team predicted Variant A would outperform; the data confirmed it.
  • Audience: Travel-intent users aged 22–45 in Malaysia, Singapore, and Thailand. "Travel-intent" is a behavioral segment ContentSquare builds from prior in-app actions (searches, hotel views, destination saves).
  • Attribution window: 7-day post-click for in-app and off-site conversions. View-through conversions counted at 50% weight.
  • Off-site connection: Pacific Airways' server-side conversion API was connected to Aeon360 two weeks before launch. Coverage at campaign end: 87% of booking events captured.
Section 4 · How to read the report

The six panels and what each one tells you

The dashboard below has six panels. Each answers a different question a brand will ask. Skim this guide once, then scroll through the dashboard with it in mind.

1

KPI strip

The "did we hit the contract" view. Every metric shown against either a plan number or a category benchmark.

2

Daily performance + audience

Pacing across time, and which user cohorts actually engaged. Brand uses this to refine targeting for the next campaign.

3

Conversion funnel

Every stage from impression to booking, with drop-off rates. Where the biggest leakage is, is where the biggest optimization lives.

4

Engagement + heatmap

ContentSquare's differentiator. Goes beyond clicks to show attention: time on creative, hover patterns, tap zones. Explains why a creative worked or didn't.

5

Creative A/B + replays

Direct comparison of variant performance, plus session replay clips showing how real users behaved. Replays turn anecdotes into evidence.

6

Off-site + LTV + AI insights

The bookings that happened on Pacific Airways' own site, the 30-day halo effect, and prioritized recommendations from the analytics copilot.

AI-generated insights & recommendations

Generated by ContentSquare's analytics copilot from this campaign's data. Three actions ranked by projected revenue impact.

🚀

1. Concentrate remaining spend on Variant A in evening hours (6–10 PM MYT)

Variant A's CTR rises to 4.8% in this window — 1.5× the day-average. Combined with KL/SG geos, projected incremental bookings: ~640, projected incremental revenue: ~$92K.

⚠️

2. Fix the date-picker friction in the Pacific Airways booking flow

9.4% of click-throughs abandon within the calendar UI (tap-rage signal detected). A fix sized at one engineering sprint would project a +11% conversion-rate uplift, equivalent to ~920 additional bookings annualized at current pacing.

📈

3. Test a "Bali + KL hotel" bundle creative for the cross-shop cohort

22% of viewers cross-shopped between Bali and other destinations. A bundle creative leveraging Aeon360's hotel inventory could capture this hesitation. Estimated cost-to-test: $4K over 7 days.

What this report achieved for Aeon360 and Pacific Airways

The campaign over-delivered, but the more interesting outcome is what the report enables next:

  • For Pacific Airways — a defensible case to renew at higher spend, plus three concrete optimizations (creative concentration, date-picker fix, bundle test) with projected dollar values.
  • For Aeon360 — evidence that justifies premium CPMs for hero placement against branded competitors. The 9.4× blended ROAS is now a reference point for every future airline pitch.
  • For Eon Credit — a 1.4% application rate from the campaign cohort is a quiet but real cross-sell signal. Worth A/B testing a credit-card overlay on next campaign.
